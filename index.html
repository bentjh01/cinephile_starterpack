1

Spirited Away

"間"
The space between the edges

This is a watercolor dreamscape where childhood innocence confronts the mysteries of the spirit world. Chihiro, adrift in a bathhouse of enchantments, grapples with greed, loss, and the bittersweet ache of growing up. The movie takes it's time to show the journey, leaving you there to breathe in the beauty of the world. A train ride across the water, walking down stairs, eating onigiri. Hayao Miyazaki, is a master of creating space.

Netflix

2

Spider-Man Into the Spider-Verse

"When will I know I'm ready?"

If there was a movie that had an epilepsy warning, it would be this. The animation in Spider-Verse is a kaleidoscope of vibrant colors, dynamic action sequences, and stunning comic book-inspired visuals.
But beneath the stunning visuals, a delicate thread of self-doubt weaves through Miles' journey. He stumbles, feels inadequate, grapples with the legacy of the mask. Yet, with each stumble, he gathers strength, embraces his own unique Spidey spirit, and proves that anyone, even a nervous Brooklyn teen, can wear the webs with pride. A love letter to the comics, it redefines "greatness" and opens a web of endless possibilities.

"You won't. Its a leap of faith"

Netflix, AppleTV

"Dogs can't look up"

Shaun of the Dead; A movie about growing up and taking responsibility for yourself and your actions. Also zombies. Edgar Wright is living very film lover's dream. He's a film lover creating films and it shows in his work. All his movies pay homage to films past. This film has that too, afterall it is called Shaun of the Dead after the classic Dawn of the Dead. Within its own world, he does this too with setups to be paid off throughout the movie.

4

Interstellar

"Why did you and mum name me after something that's bad?"

Interstellar: a cosmic symphony of grief, ambition, and the relentless quest for home. Time distorts, gravity bends, and family bonds stretch across light-years, leaving you aching for connection amidst the celestial vastness. Nolan's films showcase impossible problems like gravity-defying love and wormhole navigation, ultimately suggesting that humanity's ingenuity and compassion can conquer even the most daunting scientific riddles.o , Hans Zimmer's score stirs, and the film asks: how far will love drive us, and what sacrifices will we make for the echoes of tomorrow? In this case, love transends gravity and science will save us.

"Murphy's Law doesn't mean something bad will happen. What it means is whatever can happen, will happen"

AppleTV

5

Forrest Gump

"Momma says not to take rides from strangers"
"This is the bus to school"
"My name's Forrest, Forrest Gump"
"I'm Dorothy Harris"
"Well now we ain't strangers anymore."

There is a purity about Forrest Gump. He stumbles through history like a feather on the wind. From Vietnam jungles to ping pong tournaments in China, Forrest witnesses iconic moments with childlike wonder, reminding us of life's unexpected turns and the quiet beauty in ordinary days. Yet, beneath is not naiveté. Forrest watches love, trauma, and death around him yet maintains his purity. Choices shape our paths and Forrest chooses to be an ode to kindness, second chances, and the enduring power of hope.

"Life is like a box of chocolates. You never know what you're going to get."

Netflix, Apple TV

6

The Grand Budapest Hotel

"Give me your hand. You've nothing to fear. You're always anxious before you travel"

Prepare to be whisked away to a bygone era of European grandeur with Wes Anderson's whimsical masterpiece, The Grand Budapest Hotel. This visually stunning film follows the fastidious concierge Monsieur Gustave H (Ralph Fiennes) and his loyal lobby boy Zero Moustafa (Tony Revolori) as they navigate a web of murder, theft, and family feuds, all while preserving the elegance of their beloved hotel.

"Until I walk amongst you again as a free man, the Grand Budapest remains in your hands, as does its impeccable reputation"



Disney+

7

Whiplash

"Tell Fletcher that Im coming you motherf**ker"

Whiplash: A pulse-pounding descent into obsession. Teller smolders as Andrew, a drummer yearning for greatness under the sadistic tutelage of Simmons' drill sergeant conductor Fletcher. Every cymbal crash hits you in the gut as the line between passion and abuse blurs. Whiplash is not just a movie about drumming, it's a searing exploration of ambition, obsession, and the cost of greatness. Director Damien Chazelle crafts a cinematic symphony of sweat, blood, and blistering jazz that will leave you breathless and questioning.



8

Boyhood

"Dad - Talk to me, Samantha, how was your week?"

Boyhood: a sun-drenched tapestry of growing pains, whispered secrets, and fleeting moments strung together like dandelion wishes. Linklater's camera whispers, never judges, as Mason (Ellar Coltrane) stumbles through boyhood's labyrinth. First loves bloom and fade, parental shadows loom and recede, friendships flicker like fireflies under summer skies. It's a bittersweet ode to the liminal space between childhood and adulthood, a celebration of the ordinary that leaves you yearning for long-lost afternoons and the bittersweet sting of nostalgia.

"Dad - I dont dad. I was kinda tough. Billy and Ellen broke up and Ellen is kinda mad at me cuz she saw me talking to Billy in the cafeteria. and remember that sculpture i was working on? it was a unicorn and the horn broke off so now its a zebra"

9

Pulp Fiction

"Oh what the f**k's happening... Oh shit man"

A neon-drenched cocktail of violence, redemption, and the absurd, shaken, not stirred by Tarantino's manic genius. Travolta's hitman twirls in dance halls, Jackson's Bible verses dance on bloody floors, and Thurman's mob wife navigates a world where adrenaline is the currency and consequences dance with fate. Each twisty narrative thread, each outrageous scene, asks: where does chance end and choice begin? A darkly comic meditation on violence, loyalty, and the thin line between luck and damnation.

"Oh sh*t I shot Marvin in the face"

10

Puss in Boots: the Last Wish

"He was known across the land by many names. The Stabby Tabby. El Macho Gato. The Leche Whisperer. To some, an outlaw. To more, a hero. To all, a legend."

This movie about a talking cat has no business being ths good but it is. The animation is stellar; the adventure, exciting; And the story is captivating. It tackles themes mortality, self-reliance, and the importance of companionship. It is as if the writers wrote writing for two adiences, both children and adults. What makes this good? Because we are both audiences; we are the adults we are pretending to be and the children we are at heart.

"I know I can never defeat you, Lobo. But I will never stop fighting for this life."
